# Calibrated SMM Examples

These examples demonstrate the minimum accepted level of reasoning. Numbers are illustrative unless explicitly labeled observed; never reuse them as benchmarks.

## Example 1: Short Product/Mechanic Demo

### Brief

- `Outcome`: qualified awareness among players who value deliberate positional combat.
- `Audience evidence`: hypothetical until validated; players may read stopping before an attack as unresponsive controls.
- `Evidence label`: `Hypothesis`.
- `Content job`: prove.
- `Promise`: explain why the character plants before attacking and show the tactical benefit.
- `Proof`: two comparable encounter clips—moving while casting versus planting and casting—under named conditions.
- `Action`: view the longer combat-system breakdown.
- `Surface`: YouTube Shorts organic.
- `Forbidden claim`: “more responsive than every ARPG” without comparative data.

### Hook Candidates

| Candidate | Review | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| “You won’t believe this combat system.” | Relevance `Fail`; clarity `Fail`; proof `Fail` | Reject |
| “Почему маг замирает перед ударом?” over a hit frame | Relevance `Pass`; clarity `Weak`; specificity `Pass`; credibility `Weak`; continuity `Pass` | Improve with consequence |
| “Маг перестаёт бежать перед ударом — иначе позиционный бой ломается.” over A/B clips | Relevance `Pass`; clarity `Pass`; specificity `Pass`; credibility `Pass` if both clips are real; continuity `Pass` | Use as hypothesis |

The review is not a predictive score. It prevents known defects; performance still requires audience data.

### Beat Sheet

| Range | Viewer question | Visual | Audio/text | Function | Proof/exit risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0–2 s | What is unusual? | Split-screen: moving cast vs planted cast | “Почему маг останавливается перед ударом?” | Promise | Must be readable without knowing the game |
| 2–5 s | Why should I care? | Moving version drifts into danger | “Движение во время атаки стирает цену позиции.” | Stakes | Do not exaggerate beyond shown encounter |
| 5–10 s | What is the rule? | Cursor command, arrival, plant, attack | “Команда: добежать → закрепиться → ударить.” | Orientation | UI/cursor must remain visible |
| 10–16 s | Does it help? | Same telegraph; planted version keeps safe angle | “Теперь выбор позиции имеет последствия.” | Proof | Same encounter conditions required |
| 16–20 s | What next? | Brief system diagram or next clip | “Полный разбор управления — в профиле.” | Resolution/action | CTA only after proof |

### Measurement

- `Experiment type`: sequential organic comparison unless a platform provides concurrent allocation.
- `Primary diagnostic`: chosen-to-view and `R(5s|start)` using YouTube definitions.
- `Guardrails`: negative feedback, qualified comments about control responsiveness, click to full breakdown.
- `Decision`: a stronger opening alone is not accepted if qualified comments show the clip created a false expectation.
- `Power status`: exploratory until required sample is estimated.

## Example 2: Educational Carousel

### Brief

- `Outcome`: saves and later use by creators preparing short-form scripts.
- `Observed input required`: collect at least recurring retention questions from account comments, support, or interviews before publication.
- `Content job`: educate.
- `Promise`: distinguish a useful information gap from clickbait.
- `Proof`: definitions, paired examples, and a rewrite procedure.
- `Action`: save the checklist and apply it to one draft.

### Card Structure

1. `Hook`: “Интрига или кликбейт? Проверьте хук по пяти признакам.”
2. `Map`: subject named, missing piece bounded, payoff starts early, promise stays stable, loop closes.
3. `Bad`: “Секрет, который скрывают все…” Explain exactly which checks fail.
4. `Better`: name the subject and missing decision.
5. `Continuity`: show how card/video two begins paying off.
6. `Proof`: show claim source, demo, or limitation.
7. `Rewrite`: identify the failed dimension and change one element.
8. `Resolution`: compact checklist and proportionate “save” CTA.

### Media QA

- `Context`: organic carousel; do not cite paid-ad lift as a reach prediction.
- One idea per card; first card works alone.
- Contrast uses the internal WCAG-derived floor.
- Essential examples exist in visible text and meaningful alt text.
- Read-time heuristic is checked at phone size by a native-language reviewer.
- Export matrix includes current dimensions and crop previews from the official platform source.

## Example 3: Honest Hook Rewrite After a Dip

### Observation

- Packaging promises “three causes of low trial conversion.”
- Retention falls sharply during an eight-second biography before cause one.
- Comments do not indicate topic rejection.

### Inference Discipline

- `Observed`: location of the dip and the biography beat.
- `Hypothesis`: viewers leave because orientation is delayed.
- `Not established`: that biography is always harmful or that a specific replacement will cause improvement.

### Rewrite

- Keep audience, title, body, offer, and destination constant.
- Move the first diagnostic cause into the opening.
- Reduce credentials to a proof caption beside the relevant claim.
- Compare concurrently if the platform allows. Otherwise run a matched sequential quasi-test and report confounders.

### Decision

- Accept only if early retention improves without lowering qualified action or increasing misleading-expectation feedback.
- Report `Inconclusive` if exposure or audience composition differs materially.

## Example 5: Community Escalation

### Scenario
A comment claims a purchase caused data loss and posts an email address plus an order screenshot.

### Accepted Response
1. Preserve the incident reference without copying personal data into open planning documents.
2. Hide or restrict exposed personal information only under the approved moderation/privacy policy.
3. Acknowledge the issue without admitting an unverified cause.
4. Move the user to the approved private support route; never request more sensitive details publicly.
5. Escalate to support/privacy owner and record status.
6. If a systemic issue is confirmed, use the crisis protocol and publish verified remediation.

### Rejected Response
- Deleting criticism solely to improve sentiment.
- Debating the user before facts are checked.
- Asking for credentials, full order data, or additional personal information in public.
- Promising a fix date without owner approval.
