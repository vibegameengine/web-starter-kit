# SMM Evidence Register

## Method

This register contains 28 sources read on 2026-07-23. Preference was given to official platform documentation, peer-reviewed research, accessibility standards, and regulator guidance.

“Evidence of relevance” records a concrete finding or rule from the source and explains which SMM decision it supports. Platform guidance is treated as current, placement-specific guidance; it must be rechecked before publication. Research is transferred cautiously beyond its original population and context.

## Synthesis Rules Supported by Multiple Sources

- The opening must communicate immediately, but it must remain continuous with the message and payoff: sources 1–3, 5, 7, 8, 14.
- There is no defensible universal “ideal length”; compress to value and validate in context: sources 1, 5, 8, 15.
- Attention and engagement are intermediate signals; product-connected attention and business action decide effectiveness: sources 3, 13, 16.
- Platform-native composition and placement-specific exports matter: sources 2, 4–8.
- Captions and readable overlays are both accessibility and practical sound-off requirements: sources 5, 7, 17, 18.
- Curiosity and emotion can support attention/sharing, but must not become deception or overwhelm the intended outcome: sources 11–13.
- Distribution systems respond to audience behavior and satisfaction, not simplistic follower or watch-time hacks: sources 1, 9, 10.
- Rights, truthful claims, and conspicuous sponsorship are pre-publication gates, not post-publication cleanup: sources 19–20.
- Organic guidance must be separated from advertising playbooks and platform confounders must be recorded: sources 21 and 23.
- Sequential posting is not a true A/B test; causal claims require concurrent randomization or a defensible control design: sources 22 and 28.
- Remove extraneous stimulation from educational media and pair stories with evidence to prevent persuasive narrative from substituting for proof: sources 24–25.
- Flash safety, alternatives for essential visuals, and synthetic-media labeling belong in media preflight: sources 26–27.

## Known Evidence Gaps

- This register does not contain a sufficiently current, accessible first-party organic Instagram ranking/creative playbook. Reels advertising guidance may be used only as a production baseline; organic performance claims remain hypotheses until current official evidence or account data is available.
- Public platform documents do not expose complete ranking weights. Never convert listed signals into a deterministic “algorithm formula.”
- There is no universal evidence-backed posting time, cadence, video length, edit interval, hook score, or sample minimum. These require account-, audience-, placement-, and objective-specific evidence.
